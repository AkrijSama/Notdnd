# Inkborne Economy Law — v1

**Status:** DRAFT — Laws 1–6 owner-approved in structure; three PENDING confirms noted inline.
**Ratified:** 2026-07-11
**Owner:** Akrij

> Canon spec, pivot-layer (rulebook / world-law family). Laws constrain **structure**; numbers are owner-tunable data (see Law 6). Where this document says **PENDING**, the item is unresolved by owner ruling — it is transcribed as-is and must not be inferred or filled.

---

## LAW 1 — DUAL CURRENCY, NEVER CROSSING

- **Gold (soft):** earned in-world only (quests, sales, play). Buys in-world goods: materials, artisan stock, services, mundane gear. Gold is NEVER purchasable with real money, directly or indirectly.
- **Ink (hard, name provisional):** earned slowly in-world (achievements, milestones, earn-loop mini-game) OR purchased with real money. Buys generation-time only: custom image generation, SPOT touch-ups, premium cosmetic slots.
- **No exchange in either direction, ever.** Gold cannot buy Ink; Ink cannot buy gold or anything gold buys.

**PENDING (Gap 2):** whether gold buys existing in-world cosmetics (artisan stock) while Ink buys only custom/generated — owner confirm outstanding.

---

## LAW 2 — COSMETIC-ONLY MONETIZATION

Nothing purchasable with Ink or real money may alter any mechanical value: stats, rolls, DCs, XP, milestones, item abilities, crafting outcomes, turn economy. No convenience carve-outs touching mechanics (inventory slots, XP boosts, reroll tokens — banned). Power is earned in-world only, forever, in every world and every tier.

---

## LAW 3 — CRAFTING IS CONTRACT-ONLY THROUGH THE THREE-BAND

*(Reaffirms existing rulebook chapter.)* Schematics define WHAT can be made; materials + workstation/artisan + three-band resolution define WHETHER and HOW WELL. Crafted mechanical items obey the item law (slot + bounded stat-mods + one ability).

**PENDING (Gap 3):** schematics tradeable vs bind-on-find — owner deferred.

---

## LAW 4 — CRAFTING-AS-GENERATION

Custom cosmetic creation (artisan commissions, house decor, armor skins, player-described looks) is fictionally an artisan/workstation interaction and mechanically an image-generation event, resolved **library-first (free) → SPOT touch-up (cheap) → full generation (Ink-priced)**. The artisan NPC is the diegetic face of the generation pipeline.

---

## LAW 5 — ASSET LIBRARY RULES

Every generated image persists, part-tagged (subject, pose, expression, hair, style, world). Faces check out one-per-NPC-per-run; scenery shared freely. Curation: owner keep/toss until strangers exist; player-feedback ranking after. Schema carries from day one: **creator** field and **origin** field (`authored | generated | player-commissioned`) — empty/default now, load-bearing for the multiplayer economy.

---

## LAW 6 — TUNING SOVEREIGNTY

All rates, prices, costs, grind lengths, and drop tables are owner-tunable data — never hardcoded, never spec-fixed. Laws constrain structure; Akrij constrains numbers.

**PENDING (Gap 1):** formal confirm that mechanism-is-law / numbers-are-owner-table is the intended split.

---

## FUTURE ECOSYSTEM

*(Multiplayer-gated, design-locked, build-forbidden.)*

Player-to-player cosmetic economy — players commission items with Ink, trade/sell them to other players for gold; creator-tagged via Law 5 schema. Gold-denominated trade + Ink-denominated creation + Law 1's wall prevents real-money value extraction through the generator. Law 2 prevents power-by-proxy. Multiplayer itself remains post-validation roadmap; nothing here authorizes building it.

---

## TIER SHAPE

*(Sketch only — pricing explicitly deferred until real users exist.)*

- **Free** = full game, library-served art, slow Ink trickle.
- **Premium** = subscription, monthly Ink allowance, generation lane.
- **Forbidden** = separate rails, playerbase-gated, unchanged.
