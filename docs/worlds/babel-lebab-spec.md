# BABEL / LEBAB — World Book One: Babel

*Inkborne world book spec — full draft, mechanical + creative, for director
hard-redline.*

**Status:** built on the locked chassis (six stats, CTB queue, three-band
resolution, the Ch7 milestone track, race = boost + feat, bounded budgets,
statuses renamed-never-invented). All DIRECTOR CANON is non-negotiable and
untagged. Everything invented beyond canon is tagged **[INVENTED]** inline;
every unset mechanical value is tagged **[PROPOSED]**. Both are listed at the
end for the redline pass. This document is Book One (Babel); Book Two (Lebab)

## 1. World overview + tone bible

### 1.1 The world in one page

Earth. Ours, nearly — parallel, modern, recognizable: interstates, phone
plans, customs queues, cable news. Except that in Deep Antarctica stands the
**Tower of Babel**, one hundred floors of black obsidian, and it has *always*
been there. It predates every record that mentions it, and every record does.
Nations grew up around the fact of it the way coastlines grow up around an
ocean.

Then the **VOICE** spoke — once, worldwide, in every head at the same second
— and beckoned a champion. Until that champion reclaims the Tower, **chaos
seeps from it**: a corruption that soaks into flora and fauna at the world's
thin places and turns them feral. Around the seep-zones, humanity has built
what it always builds — fences, agencies, licensing regimes, and a
professional class of **awakened hunters** ranked E through SSS, managed by
national **associations** whose politics collide over the three things worth
colliding over: tower access, chaos-zone salvage, and floor-rights.

The player is the champion — though nobody, including the player, gets told
that in those words. In the real world they were poisoned and lie in a coma.
Their soul crossed. They wake in a corrupted frontier forest near a small
starter town, an ocean and a hemisphere away from Antarctica, with nothing
but a body, the VOICE, and a **STATUS WINDOW** no one else can see. The road
to the Tower runs through a training arc, three associations' colliding
wants, and a displayed **Level 50** gate at the Tower door.

At the Tower's summit, a hundred floors up, waits a sub-boss and a secret:
Babel is a hinge. Below it, inverted, descend the thousand floors of
**LEBAB**. That is Book Two, and this spec keeps it sealed (§8).

### 1.2 Tone bible — how Babel sounds

**Register: contemporary, declarative, unimpressed.** The narrator writes
like a good modern thriller, not a fantasy novel. Short sentences.
Infrastructure nouns: chain-link, diesel, drone hum, laminated licenses,
customs stamps. People say "zone," "cordon," "core," "assessment" — nobody
says "realm," "quest," or "adventurer." The world has had the Tower forever;
it is not amazed by it, the way port cities are not amazed by the sea.
**[INVENTED — all tone rules in 1.2 are invented register law]**

**Awe is rationed.** The narrator spends wonder in exactly two places: the
VOICE and the Tower. Everything else — beasts, skills, salvage, even death —
is handled in working prose. When the register suddenly lifts, the player
should feel the pressure change.

**The WINDOW speaks the numbers; the narrator never does.** Stats, levels,
ranks, XP — those appear only in the diegetic STATUS WINDOW (§2.3), rendered
as UI text. Narration describes what numbers *feel* like ("the swing lands
harder than your arm should allow") and leaves the arithmetic to the pane.
This is the Ch1 referee split, worn diegetically: the engine has a voice in
this world, and it isn't the narrator's.

**The VOICE is typographically distinct and never paraphrased.** It renders
in bracketed small-cap lines (below). The narrator may describe its weight,
never restate its content. It does not joke, comfort, or hurry.

**Chaos texture, not gore.** Corruption reads as *wrongness*: geometry that
disagrees with itself, a green too green, air with signal-static in it,
animals moving on the wrong beats. The horror is in physics misbehaving, not
in viscera.

**Inside the Tower, the register ages.** Past the Tower door, sentences
lengthen, cadence slows, vocabulary goes older — the one place the modern
voice gives way, because the Tower predates every language including the
narrator's. The contrast is the paintbrush stroke: players should *hear*
the threshold.

**Banned strokes:** high-diction fantasy filler outside the Tower;
the narrator quoting mechanics; the VOICE paraphrased, chatty, or frequent
(it speaks at commits and thresholds, never small talk); Solo-Leveling
tropes named aloud (no NPC says "it's like that manhwa").

## 2. The Opening

Per the locked GM-pacing law: turn-1 delivers the lore in-fiction via a
quest-giver. In Babel, **the quest-giver is the VOICE**, and the lore-dump is
the beckoning itself.

### 2.1 Cold open (pre-choice narration frame)

The run opens on the last seconds of the old life: a restaurant, a bitter
aftertaste, a floor tilting up — **the poisoning is narrated in four
sentences, no names, no investigation hook left open in Book One**
[INVENTED — the poisoning stays an unopened wound; Book Two material]. Then
dark. Then forest floor, wet needles against a cheek, and a body standing up
that is theirs and isn't.

The player wakes on the **fringe of the Green Static** (§2.5), at dawn,
unarmed, in civilian clothes that are not the ones they died in. One turn of
free observation (Tier-0; observation never rolls) — and then the VOICE.

### 2.2 The VOICE's turn-1 delivery — the actual words

Rendered in its own typography; drafted for production use
**[INVENTED — exact wording, for redline]**:

> [ YOU ARE HEARD. ]
>
> [ THIS WORLD IS CALLED WHAT YOURS WAS CALLED. IT IS OLDER THAN IT LOOKS.
> AT ITS SOUTH POLE STANDS A TOWER OF ONE HUNDRED FLOORS. IT HAS ALWAYS
> STOOD. IT IS CALLED BABEL, AND IT HAS BEEN LEFT UNATTENDED TOO LONG. ]
>
> [ WHAT LEAKS FROM AN UNATTENDED TOWER IS CALLED CHAOS. IT SOAKS INTO
> GREEN THINGS AND RUNNING THINGS AND MAKES THEM WRONG. THE PEOPLE HERE
> FENCE IT, LICENSE IT, AND SELL TICKETS TO IT. THEY ARE MANAGING THE
> SYMPTOM. YOU ARE THE CURE. ]
>
> [ YOUR BODY DIED ELSEWHERE. YOUR SOUL DID NOT. I HAVE PLACED IT WHERE IT
> CAN GROW. YOUR FLESH HERE IS MORTAL — GUARD IT. WHAT YOU ARE IS NOT —
> BUT THERE ARE THINGS BELOW THE WORLD THAT EAT WHAT YOU ARE, AND YOU WILL
> MEET THEM LAST. ]
>
> [ CLIMB. RECLAIM WHAT WAS LEFT OPEN. I WILL NOT SPEAK OFTEN. I HAVE GIVEN
> YOU A WINDOW SO THAT YOU NEED NOT TAKE MY WORD FOR ANYTHING. ]
>
> [ NORTH OF YOU: A TOWN OF THE LICENSED. AROUND YOU: THE WRONG WOODS.
> BOTH TEACH. CHOOSE. ]

Six beats, and each is load-bearing: **the land** (parallel Earth, the
seep), **the tower** (Babel, Antarctica, always-existed, unattended), **the
role** (champion/cure, climb and reclaim), **the soul law** (mortal body,
unharmable soul, a last-things warning that seeds Lebab), **the WINDOW
grant**, and **the choice**. This is the world's onboarding beat: no
worldgen fields, no menu — the VOICE *is* the character-facing onboarding.

### 2.3 The STATUS WINDOW grant

Immediately after the VOICE's last line, the WINDOW manifests — the

Only the Beckoned has a live WINDOW. The rest of the world measures rank

offices — which read the same truths more slowly and less completely
[INVENTED — assessment rigs; keeps the WINDOW unique without breaking the
world's rank economy]. Diegetically, the WINDOW is the VOICE's gift;
mechanically, it is the character sheet, the rank readout (§5), and the
milestone tracker (§6), and it renders whenever the sheet would.

### 2.4 The choice: forest or town

The first player decision, offered plainly and honored hard. Both paths
teach the same chassis; they teach it in different orders
[INVENTED — path design]:

- **The town road (soft opening).** Two hours' walk north to Hollow Pine
  (§2.5). Teaches: safe conversation never rolls, licensing and money, the
  job board, the cast. First combat comes chosen, not ambushed — a posted
  fringe-clearance contract. The Charter, Assembly, and Office fronts (§3)
  all have their first telegraphs waiting in town.
- **The forest deep (hard opening).** Turning away from the road, into the
  Static. Teaches: the check economy under pressure, first blood (a fringe
  beast, E-threat), the salvage verb (the drone cache), and — if the player
  keeps going — Warden Calloway, the training-arc mentor (§2.6). Harder,
  faster, richer in early skills; the town is still there afterward.

Neither path gates content; they order it. The engine records the choice as
the run's first committed event (it pays the ledger's first XP).

### 2.5 Starter region sketch — two locations

**Hollow Pine** [INVENTED — town, name, cast] — pop. ~1,400, an old timber
town an hour past the end of reliable cell coverage, now a licensed frontier
outpost making uneasy money off the zone it lives beside. Chain-link and
prefab around a Victorian-era main street. Diesel generators. A flagpole
with three flags: national, state, and the hunter-association pennant.

Cast seeds:
- **Marshal Odile Grace** — the Charter's desk officer: licenses, contracts,
  assessments by appointment. By-the-book, quietly decent, first to say the
  words "you're not licensed, so legally you're a bystander."
- **Tobias "Saw" Rourke** — ex-D-rank, one arm, runs **the Stump** (bar,
  job board, rumor exchange). The soft opening's quest-giver texture; knows
  everyone's business including the associations'.
- **Priya Vail** — salvage broker, Charter-adjacent, morally flexible; buys
  cores and zone-salvage at 60 cents on the dollar and knows it. First rung
  of the Salvage Clause front runs through her ledger.
- **Doc Emory Han** — frontier medic, quietly feeding seep-readings to the
  Quiet Office. Patches hunters; asks questions that are someone else's.

**The Green Static (Exclusion Zone EZ-44)** [INVENTED — zone, name,
contents] — a corrupted stretch of Pacific-Northwest rainforest. From the
road it reads as weather: the air above the canopy shimmers like bad signal.
Inside, moss grows in Fibonacci spirals it shouldn't know, elk move on wrong
beats, and compass needles agree with each other but not with the sun.
Fringe threat E; deepens to D/C toward the heart.

Location seeds:
- **The drone cache** — a downed Charter survey drone, salvage-legal in
  precisely nobody's opinion. First loot verb; first Salvage Clause hook.
- **The Stag** — the zone's corrupted alpha, a C-threat at the heart, the
  training arc's graduation fight; its core is worth a season of a broker's
  patience.
- **Warden Ruth Calloway** — ex-B-rank, off-grid inside the zone for nine
  years, the only person who treats the Static as a neighbor rather than a
  resource or a threat. The training-arc mentor: teaches the first D-rank
  skills, and is the one NPC who guesses, early and privately, what the
  player's WINDOW means.

### 2.6 Training arc, then travel — the Book One spine

Canon shape: **train on the frontier → cross a hostile political map →
enter the Tower at displayed Level 50 (milestone 11) → climb to the
floor-100 reveal.** The three association fronts (§3) are the travel
pressure: each one is simultaneously an obstacle, a patron, and a road to
Antarctica, and the chaos-seep front (§3.5) is the clock under all of it.

## 3. The three associations + the standing front

Regional texture law [INVENTED — tendency mechanics]: each association's
territory tilts its hunter NPCs' skill tendencies (a generation weight, not
a rule on players — spawn tables weight these stats/skill-tags for NPC
hunters of that association).

### 3.1 THE CHARTER — United States [INVENTED — name, character, front]

*The American Hunters Charter, HQ Denver.* Salvage capitalism with a
flag on it. Contractor culture, gear-first doctrine, the best logistics on
Earth and the honest belief that monetizing the apocalypse is how you pay to
fight it. **Tendencies:** DEX/INT; firearm- and rig-keyed skills; item
synergies. **The concrete want: exclusive salvage rights to chaos zones —
"the cores pay for the war."**

**Front: THE SALVAGE CLAUSE** — kind: opportunity/danger, D.5-shaped.
- *Grounded in:* Hollow Pine, EZ-44, Priya Vail, Marshal Grace, the drone
  cache.
- *Agenda:* push a federal rider — the Salvage Clause — privatizing
  exclusion-zone salvage; EZ-44 is the pilot.
- **Rung 1 — Survey.** Charter drones over the Static; Vail's buying prices
  suddenly double. *Telegraph: contrails and cash.*
- **Rung 2 — The Auction.** EZ-44 salvage rights announced for closed bid;
  Grace ordered to freeze independent licenses. *Decision: get licensed
  before the window shuts, or get useful to a bidder.*
- **Rung 3 — The Fence.** Contractor fencing goes up across the zone
  fringe; Hollow Pine's economy chokes; locals start cutting wire.
- **Rung 4 — The Clearance.** A contractor deep-clearance op hits the
  Stag's territory and breaks it open — a beast wave rolls at the town
  (hands the escalation to §3.5). *Resolution paths: the Clause dies in
  scandal / passes and the town hollows / passes amended with local
  carve-outs the player brokered.*

### 3.2 THE SEOUL ASSEMBLY — South Korea [INVENTED — name, character, front]

*HQ Seoul.* Raid meritocracy and national mythology: the Assembly fields
the highest S-rank density on Earth and treats the Tower the way a summit
nation treats an unclimbed peak. Its top hunters are celebrities with
agencies and endorsement conflicts. **Tendencies:** STR/DEX; close-combat
prodigy skills; the deepest bench of high-rank melee on the planet. **The
concrete want: first-entry floor-rights — the Assembly's flag on every
floor nobody has mapped, and a recognized claim regime that makes
first-clear mean ownership.**

**Front: THE GATE QUEUE** — kind: rivalry/opportunity, D.5-shaped.
- *Grounded in:* the Antarctica staging port (McMurdo-adjacent
  [INVENTED: "Gate Station Aleph"]), an Assembly advance team transiting
  the starter region, the Stump's job board.
- *Agenda:* force an international first-entry queue with Assembly primacy
  before anyone else's champion-tier hunters are ready.
- **Rung 1 — The Scout.** An Assembly advance team passes through Hollow
  Pine, assessing frontier talent; they clock the player's growth curve as
  statistically wrong. *Telegraph: a business card and a too-long look.*
- **Rung 2 — The Queue.** The Assembly announces the Gate Queue accord;
  signatory associations get numbered entry slots. Independents get none.
- **Rung 3 — The Collision.** Charter contractors and Assembly raiders
  face off at Gate Station Aleph over slot enforcement; the news cycle
  turns the Tower into an election issue on three continents.
- **Rung 4 — The Offer.** The Assembly offers the player a queue slot — in
  exchange for a binding raid contract under their flag. *Resolution
  paths: sign (patron road to the gate) / refuse and find another door /
  break the queue itself (the hard, loud path).*

### 3.3 THE QUIET OFFICE — Japan [INVENTED — name, character, front]

*Seimon-kyoku, "the Quiet Office," Tokyo.* Containment doctrine embodied:
wards, cordons, and the institutional memory of a nation that has already
lost a city district to a seep bloom [INVENTED — the Kiyosu Loss, twelve
years back; never shown, always felt]. The Office believes every zone
opened is a wound widened — including, especially, the Tower. **Tendencies:**
Spirit/INT; ward-, silence-, and shield-flavored skills; the world's best
containment specialists and almost no celebrity hunters at all. **The
concrete want: global cordon authority — recognized power to close any
zone, gate, or expedition it deems seep-critical. Including Babel.**

**Front: THE QUIET CORDON** — kind: danger/secret, D.5-shaped.
- *Grounded in:* EZ-44 seep-readings, Doc Emory Han, Hollow Pine,
  the zone heart.
- *Agenda:* prove EZ-44 is pre-bloom and close it — with the town inside
  the line if the math says so.
- **Rung 1 — The Readings.** Office assessors quietly resurvey the Static;
  Han's clinic data goes somewhere new. *Telegraph: strangers with
  excellent manners and unmarked equipment.*
- **Rung 2 — The Leak.** The cordon proposal leaks: EZ-44 *and Hollow
  Pine* inside the line. The town splits; the Charter smells a rival
  land-grab; the Assembly doesn't care.
- **Rung 3 — The Notices.** Evacuation notices post. Grace must choose
  which law she serves this month.
- **Rung 4 — The Line.** The cordon closes — unless the player has changed
  the inputs. *The secret rung:* **the Office's math is right.** Something
  under the Static is waking (the Stag is a symptom, not the disease), and
  every other faction is arguing about property. *Resolution paths: the
  cordon lands and the town dies politely / the player clears the
  under-threat and the readings fall / the bloom comes and everyone learns
  what Kiyosu meant.*

**The collision triangle:** the Charter wants to *mine* EZ-44, the Office
wants to *entomb* it, and the Assembly wants everyone's best hunters at the
*gate queue* instead of wasting a season on a frontier zone. All three
fronts ground in the starter region, so the world's politics are playable
from session one — and all three roads lead, by patronage or defiance, to
Antarctica.

### 3.5 THE SEEP — standing world-pressure front (canon)

Kind: danger, **standing** — it cannot fully resolve in Book One; only the
Tower's reclamation ends it. D.5-shaped with an escalation clock instead of
a linear ladder:

- *Grounded in:* every exclusion zone on the map; in the starter region,
  EZ-44, the Stag, the under-threat of §3.3.
- *Agenda:* the Tower is unattended; chaos finds the thin places.
- **L0 — Ambient.** Flora/fauna corruption inside zones (the standing
  texture; feral beasts are the world's weather).
- **L1 — Sightings.** Corrupted beasts outside the wire; livestock and
  outbuildings first.
- **L2 — The Wave.** A coordinated-feeling beast push at a settlement
  (Hollow Pine's palisade scene).
- **L3 — Growth.** A zone eats map: the exclusion boundary formally moves,
  and someone's home is now inside.
- **L4 — Calamity.** A named calamity-class beast emerges (regional
  boss-front; the world holds its breath).
- *Clock law [PROPOSED]:* the level rises with world time and with
  unattended active zones; committed clears (beast dens, under-threats,
  zone hearts) tick it down locally. The front's global level gates other
  fronts' rungs (e.g., Cordon Rung 4 auto-fires anywhere local seep hits
  L3). Constants for the clock are validator data, tunable per region.

## 4. Power system

### 4.1 Awakening lore

Nobody is born awakened. The seep that corrupts green things does something
else in people: in roughly one in ten thousand, exposure *tunes* the soul
instead of fouling the body, and a hunter wakes up [INVENTED — awakening
rate and mechanism]. Awakening manifests as the six stats becoming *load
bearing* — a hunter's STR, Spirit, INT are the same qualities civilians
have, turned up past what flesh alone explains. Skills (§4.3) are learned
disciplines of the awakened state; associations run academies, assessments,
and licensing on top of the biology like nations always do.

### 4.2 The race slot: AWAKENING ORIGINS

One species, modern Earth — so Babel fills the chassis's race slot with
**how you awakened**. The origin contract is exactly the chassis race
contract: **a stat boost + one feat (a rank-E skill), inside the universal
race budget** [PROPOSED — origin budget: +1 to each of two designated
stats, plus the listed E-skill as the origin feat].

| Origin | Who awakens this way | Boost | Origin feat |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Gate-Touched** | Civilians who survived a seep event | +1 VIT, +1 Spirit | Static Sense |
| **Academy-Forged** | Association-trained since testing age | +1 DEX, +1 INT | Deadeye |
| **Deep-Blood** | Hunter families, second and third generation | +1 STR, +1 VIT | Ironhide |
| **Dream-Called** | Woke already awakened, out of an ordinary sleep | +1 Spirit, +1 Luck | Second Wind |
| **Static-Born** | Born or raised inside a zone's fringe | +1 DEX, +1 Luck | Haggler's Tongue |
| **The Beckoned** | *One soul, placed by the VOICE* | +1 INT, +1 Spirit | The STATUS WINDOW itself |

[INVENTED — all six origins, names and assignments.] **The Beckoned is
campaign-reserved** (the player); its "feat" is the live WINDOW plus the
250-cap gift (§6) — unique by canon, not by budget. The other five are the
world's population — which makes them the **host-generation table** for the
death profile (§7): when the soul is reassigned, the new body has one of
these five origins.

### 4.3 Skills = feats, ranked E → DG

Skills are this world's feats: named, learnable, tagged with a rank, and
budget-bound. **Rank ↔ chassis feat budget mapping** [PROPOSED]:

| Rank | E | D | C | B | A | S | SS | SSS | DG |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Budget units | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 | 8 | **10** |
| Index (for §5) | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 | 8 | 9 |

Effect costs draw from the engine vocabulary only (the ten statuses, queue
operations, edge/burden, attack verbs, derived-stat bonuses) at a
validator-priced table [PROPOSED — cost table: one-shot edge/burden = 1;
bounded status application = 1 per bounded turn of it; granted attack verb
= 2; permanent +1 to a derived stat = 3; queue-weight manipulation = 1–2 by
scope; self-cost riders discount 1]. The **DG jump to 10** is deliberate:
DG skills are a different kind of thing (they touch souls — §7), they are
**player-reachable only at the final milestones (19–20)**, and **no DG
skill instance ships in Book One** — the tier exists in the contract, its
content lives in deep Lebab.

**The world skews low** (canon). Population rank distribution
[PROPOSED — the numbers; the skew itself is canon]:

> E 55% · D 25% · C 12% · B 5% · A 2.2% · S 0.7% · SS 0.09% · SSS 0.01% ·
> DG — never yet held by a living hunter.

A C-rank hunter is a solid professional. A single A-rank changes a
regional labor market. An S-rank is national news when they fly. This
table also drives NPC generation and death-profile host selection (§7).

## 5. Hunter RANK — the amalgamation formula [PROPOSED]

Canon: rank is a server-computed weighted amalgamation of owned skill
ranks; outliers pull upward; 2×E + 1×A ≈ C. **Proposed formula: the
root-mean-square of skill rank indices** (E=1 … DG=9, table §4.3):

> **rankIndex = √( mean( index(skillᵢ)² ) )**, displayed as the letter at
> **round(rankIndex)**.

The quadratic mean *is* the canon behavior: squaring weights high-rank
skills disproportionately, so outliers drag the aggregate up — and the
canon example lands exactly:

| Skills held | Mean of squares | √ | Displayed |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2×E + 1×A *(canon test)* | (1+1+25)/3 = 9 | **3.00** | **C** ✓ |
| 3×E | 1 | 1.00 | E (junk can't inflate) |
| 4×E + 1×S | (4+36)/5 = 8 | 2.83 | C (one S among junk = C) |
| 2×C + 2×B | (9+9+16+16)/4 = 12.5 | 3.54 | B |
| 1×S only | 36 | 6.00 | S (one skill can define you) |

Zero skills = **UNASSESSED** (the WINDOW's word for it). The WINDOW shows
the letter plus the raw index to one decimal (`RANK C (3.0)`) with a
progress bar to the next letter boundary (boundaries at x.50, standard
rounding); association assessment rigs show the letter only — one more
thing the WINDOW knows that the world doesn't [INVENTED — display split].

## 6. Milestone mapping — displayed 1–250 [PROPOSED]

Per the chassis milestone law (Ch7): ~20 milestones carry all mechanical
growth; the world book maps them to **breakthrough levels**; everything
between is minor levels (HP tick + fiction only); tier gates read
milestones, never display numbers.

| Milestone | Displayed level | Tier | World meaning |
|---:|---:|---|---|
| 1 | 1 | I | Awakening confirmed |

## 7. The death-profile law

Babel's death profile implements the canon SOUL LAW on the universal Ch8
machinery. The body is fully mortal: death saves, instant-death overflow,
stabilization — all of Ch8, full combat stakes, no exceptions. The *soul*
is a separate register that normal means cannot touch. Only DG-tier skills
and the God can affect a soul. Four exits from a run; the first is not a
game over at all:

### 7.1 Natural body-death → soul reassignment (the world's death law)

When the body dies (Ch8: three failed saves, or instant death), the soul
does not. **The world's god** — this book identifies the God and the VOICE
as one entity wearing two functions: the VOICE is how it speaks, the God is
how it acts [INVENTED — the identification; the entity's book-name is
**the Custodian**] — **reassigns the soul into an existing, compatible,
weak-souled hunter of random rank.**

**Compatibility, in-world** [INVENTED — the justification]: reassignment
targets hunters in **soul-fade** — a documented chaos affliction that hollows
the self out of a living body (associations classify it as career-ending;
families grieve it as a living death). The Custodian does not evict anyone;
it fills a house the tenant already left. This keeps the death law playable
without making the player a body-thief.

**What carries over (soul-side):**
- The player's knowledge — everything known, learned, suspected. The
  server keeps the full canon/journal; nothing the player learned is
  revoked.
- The VOICE bond, the STATUS WINDOW, and the 250-cap gift — the WINDOW
  re-manifests on the new body's first waking turn (the diegetic signal
  that the champion continues).
- Quest knowledge and standing objectives — threads re-key to the new
  body; fronts do not reset.

**What does not (body-side):**
- Stats, skills, milestone position, HP, gear-in-slots: the sheet becomes
  **the host's sheet**, as it exists — the host's origin (one of the five,
  §4.2), the host's skills, the host's milestone. The prior body's
  possessions lie where it fell (recoverable the hard way; the world does
  not courier your estate).

**The server hand-over flow:**
1. Death commits (Ch8 machinery completes).
2. **The antechamber** — a no-roll narrated interstitial in the Custodian's
   presence; one VOICE beat, cold and brief; the reassignment is decreed,
   never negotiated [INVENTED — the antechamber scene shape].
3. Host selection: an existing seeded hunter NPC flagged soul-faded if one
   is live in the region; else the server **generates** the host — rank
   drawn from the world distribution (§4.3: heavily low; a returning
   champion is usually an E- or D-rank body), origin from the five, skills
   budget-legal for the rank, placed at that hunter's real location with
   that hunter's real entanglements.
4. **The world persists and remembers** (canon): the prior hunter's deeds
   stay canon — NPCs mourn, fronts advanced by the dead hunter stay
   advanced, the name goes on the Stump's wall [INVENTED — the wall]. The
   new host's own history (debts, contracts, a sister in Busan) seeds the
   next arc's hooks.
5. First waking turn: the WINDOW renders on the new sheet. Play continues.
   The run does not end; the *body* did.

```json
  "carryOver": ["journal","canon","voiceBond","statusWindow","capGift","threads"],
  "reset": ["stats","skills","milestone","hp","inventorySlots"],
  "interstitial": "antechamber_scene"
}
```

### 7.2 Soul Prison — the god-tier sentence and the timeskip

For transgressions at the God's own scale [INVENTED — what earns it: raising
a hand against the Custodian's decree, or DG-tier soul-crimes], the sentence
is the **Soul Prison**: 1,000–10,000 years, decreed not rolled. The prison
sits in the Tower's own obsidian — **the Oubliette floors**, unnumbered
spaces between numbered ones [INVENTED — location].

The sentence is served as a **world-altering timeskip** (canon): when the
soul emerges, **everything has regenerated except the Tower.** In engine
terms: all procedural surface content re-rolls — towns, associations,
borders, NPCs, fronts, the entire §2–§3 layer re-seeds from the world's
generators; the old world is archived as canon-history (the WINDOW dates
it: "the Old Age") and surfaces only as ruins, records, and legends. **The
Tower's authored floors persist exactly** — Babel and Lebab are the world's
one fixed spine across any skip; the Tower does not care what century is
knocking (canon: authored content persists, the outside world re-rolls).

**Two rescue shapes, both real quests:**
- **The hunter's rescue** — a living hunter reaches the Oubliette: a
  multi-front chain (locate the unnumbered floor → survive the approach →
  break or bargain the seal). In solo play this shape fires when the
  *player* is the rescuer of a sentenced NPC — and it establishes,
  on-screen, that the door can be opened, so a sentenced *player* knows
  rescue is a real mechanic, not flavor [INVENTED — quest shape].
- **The God's commutation** — the Custodian commutes the sentence for a
  **geas**: release bound to a divine objective, unremovable, tracked as a
  foreground thread until discharged. The VOICE states terms once; the
  terms do not improve [INVENTED — quest shape].

### 7.3 Banishment — the living exile (diegetic game over)

A God-tier expulsion: the soul is sent **home** — the coma ends, the player
character wakes in the real world, alive and permanently sick from the
poison [canon]. The run ends there, diegetically: a short epilogue in the
real world's gray — survived, diminished, and locked out of the world where
they mattered [INVENTED — epilogue shape]. Recorded as a completed run
(outcome: banished), not a death.

### 7.4 Vanquishment — true death (the worst end)

Only DG-tier or the God can destroy a soul [canon]. Vanquishment is the
true game over: no reassignment, no home, no epilogue narrator except the
world itself — the run archives, and the world's canon records that the
champion ended, and where [INVENTED — the world keeps a public grave-record;
future runs in the same world may find it]. The rarest end in the design:
nothing below deep Lebab can even attempt it — which is the §2.2 warning:
"you will meet them last."

### 8.1 Babel, floors 1–100 — floor bands as content tiers

The Tower is authored content (the fixed spine that survives timeskips),
banded to the milestone track. Entry gate: **displayed Level 50 —

### 8.2 Floor 100 — the reveal

At the summit waits the floor-100 sub-boss — **the Mirrored Warden**
[INVENTED — name; canon — its existence and function]: not the Tower's
master but its *doorman*, the Custodian's inverted reflection. Beating it
does not end the game. It ends the *premise*: the summit is a hinge, and
what the Warden was guarding is **down** — **LEBAB**, the Tower inverted,
**one thousand floors descending** below the hundred everyone could see.
The climb was the tutorial. The world's real depth is a mirror-shaft, and
everything the VOICE left unsaid in §2.2 ("things below the world that eat
what you are — you will meet them last") is down there.

### 8.3 The Book One / Book Two boundary

**Book One (this spec): the surface world + Babel 1–100.** It ships: the
opening, the frontier, the three association fronts, the Seep, the travel
arc, the gate at milestone 11, the climb, and the floor-100 reveal as its
final authored beat. Book One contains **zero DG skill instances**, no
Lebab floors, and no soul-eating antagonists — vanquishment exists in law
(§7.4) but nothing in Book One can perform it.

**Book Two (sealed): LEBAB — 1000 descending floors.** Committed hooks
Book One must honor and may not spend: the descent structure; DG-tier
skills as deep-Lebab content takeable at milestones 19–20; the
soul-eaters; the Custodian's seat and the truth of the VOICE; the
poisoning (the unopened wound of §2.1) [INVENTED — that the poisoning
resolves in Book Two]. The floor-100 reveal is the only Lebab content Book
One renders. Everything below the hinge is out of this spec's scope by
design — the sequel hook is authored; the sequel is not.
